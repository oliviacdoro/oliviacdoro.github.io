Panhellenic Council

The University of Northern Colorado's Panhellenic Council is home to five chapters; Alpha Omicron Pi, Alpha Phi, Alpha Sigma Alpha, Delta Zeta, and Sigma Kappa. The Panhellenic Community aims to provide philanthropic, leadership, and social opportunities to young women so that they can grow throughout their collegiate experience.

The first step in California's plan is reducing or ultimately eliminating the worst culprits, including single-use plastics, synthetic fabrics, cigarette filters and car tires. The idea to start small will soon be common sense, especially as California leads the nation in environmental and sustainability protection efforts like ending the sale of gas cars by 2035. It is these small steps that will create a larger social impact, first in the U.S. and then beyond. The Earth is 71% water-covered, and the damage humanity has done has already left it's mark on ocean treasures like the Great Barrier Reef to now extinct ocean species. Removing plastic from the oceans is no longer the top priority for ocean ocnservation, but eliminating the need and use of single-use plastic is. California's initiative is tackling the perspective from a modern view and puts the most urgent needs first, which will help preserve the beauty of the ocean for everyone.

The plan also commits to monitoring levels of microplastics in California’s waters, just as the state monitors the level of harmful particulate matter in the air, which can often increase during events like wildfires.
